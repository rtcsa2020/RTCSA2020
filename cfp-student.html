The RTCSA conference series (now at its 26th edition) brings together researchers and developers from academia and industry for advancing the technology and the theory to design and develop time-sensitive applications. The primary purpose of the student session is to provide students with an opportunity to discuss their ideas and gather feedback from conference participants. Students can present their preliminary work on real-time systems,CPS,IoT,embedded systems,fog/edge/cloud computing. Proceedings will be published by the IEEE Computer Society on IEEExplore and will be indexed by most search engines including Scopus, Web of Science, DBLP.

 

Submission Guidelines

Students in all areas of Real-time systems, CPS, (Industrial) IoT, embedded systems, fog/edge/cloud computing areas are invited to submit a student session paper. The first author of the submission should be student and a student author should present the paper in person. The submitted manuscript must describe original work not previously published and not concurrently submitted elsewhere. The submitted manuscript must describe original work not previously published and not concurrently submitted elsewhere. The submission must be formatted according to the IEEE conference proceedings format(two-columns, single-space, 10pt) and must include references and acknowledgements within the 2 page limit. By submitting a paper, authors implicitly agree that at least one author (at least student registration rate) will register to the conference and participate by presenting the paper in person. RTCSA 2020 reserves the right to exclude a paper from distribution after the conference (e.g., removal from the digital libraries and indexing services), if the paper is not presented at the conference.

Special Notice - COVID-19

The safety and well-being of all conference participants is our top priority. After studying and evaluating the announcements, guidance, and news released by relevant national departments, we have made the decision that RTCSA 2020 will continue as scheduled on 19-21 August, by being converted to an exciting, fully virtual conference, and will not take place physically in Korea.

Registration Fee

We require at least one paid registration per accepted paper. The Organizing Committee will be making a final determination on a new, reduced flat registration fee for such registrations. All other registrations will be free.

Virtual Conference Format

We are currently evaluating many factors and reviewing best practices to determine the optimal format for the various sessions that are scheduled at RTCSA 2020. We will be making a relevant announcement as soon as these details are finalized. We hope you all stay safe and well, and look forward to working with you all to put on an exciting virtual RTCSA.
